------------------------------Kafka-Interview Questions-----------------------

1 : what is message broker ? 
ans : 

A message broker is middleware that acts as a middleman between the producer and the consumer. It receives messages from the producer, stores them temporarily, and delivers them to the consumer. Kafka is one example of a message broker.

Q: What is the purpose of Kafka? Why do we use Kafka? Where have you used it in your project?

Answer:

"The purpose of Kafka is to enable asynchronous communication between applications or microservices. Without Kafka, one service calls another service directly and waits for the response before continuing its work. With Kafka, the producer sends the message to Kafka and immediately continues its own work without waiting. The consumer reads and processes the message independently. This improves performance, scalability, reliability, and keeps the services loosely coupled."

Where did you use Kafka in your Card Management project?

"In my Card Management project, we used Kafka for communication between microservices. Whenever a card was created, activated, blocked, or updated, the Card Management Service published an event to Kafka. It did not wait for the Notification Service or Audit Service. These services consumed the event from Kafka and performed their work independently. This made the application faster, more reliable, and loosely coupled."
